---

After a yearlong residency exploring climate change, drought, fire disturbances, and the renewing rhythms of landscapes <i>flux</i>ART was brought to life at Oregon State University's Patricia Valian Reser Center for the Creative Arts (PRAx).

Artists and FLUXNET scientists gathered in Corvallis, OR 21-23 January, 2026 to celebrate <i>flux</i>ART, drawing several hundreds of visitors to experience their work at the opening event of PRAx’s exhibition. <a href ="https://prax.oregonstate.edu/visual-arts/past-exhibitions/currents-experiments-art-science-collaboration">Currents: Experiments in Art-Science Collaboration</a> featured ten international artists, five of which engaged with FLUXNET research and scientists via the <a href = "https://fluxnetart.github.io/"><i>flux</i>ART</a> residency directed by Maoya Bassiouni as well as <a href = "http://climatewhirl.fi/en">Climate Whirl</a> Art&Science Program and <a href = "https://www.periferia.helsinki.fi/">Periferia Forest Art Lab</a> curated by Ulla Taipale.

Events kicked off with a field visit led by scientist Chad Hanson (Oregon State University) to the Metolius old aged ponderosa pine Ameriflux tower (US-Me4), a key research site where artist Julia Oldham collected field recordings and drone imagery during her residency, particularly for her 4-channel video project <a href = "https://fluxnetart.github.io/pondering/">Pondering</a>. 


Prof. Timo Vesala (University of Helsinki) gave a visual lecture weaving together personal anecdotes and reflections with film excerpts at the Center for the Future of Forests and Society on 23 January. This lecture <a href = "https://events.oregonstate.edu/event/cffs-speaks-lecture-reception-with-timo-vesala">Treebound – Puuhunsidotut</a>, first commissioned for the <a href = "https://www.periferia.helsinki.fi/">Periferia Forest Art Lab</a>, explores the life of trees and their relationship to humans as Timo tries to find answers to pressing environmental, social, and economic questions about forests through cinematography.

Julia Oldham’s immersive audiovisual installation <a href = "https://fluxnetart.github.io/orange/">September: Orange</a> was on view in the Black Box Ray Theater 5 - 24 January, 2026, inviting visitors on a journey through PhenoCam timelapses across 24 sites increasingly marked by wildfire. 

The premiere of Sara Bouchard’s electroacoustic composition <a href = "https://news.vcu.edu/article/2026/03/vcuarts-professor-gives-voice-to-a-single-atoms-journey"><i>En Masse</i></a> was performed on 22 January, 2026 by the Oregon State Vocal Ensemble and Parker Williams in Detrick Concert Hall. Sara’s piece sings the carbon cycle, from air, wood, soil, fire, sonifying rising atmospheric CO2 data and dissolving climate anxiety into shared breath.

Four <i>flux</i>ART projects were exhibited in the Stirek Gallery: <a href = "https://fluxnetart.github.io/yenhaku/">Yanhaku (Breath in)</a> (yucca plant fibers, natural pigments, natural clay) by Mallery Quetawki; <a href = "https://fluxnetart.github.io/biospherevideo/">Breathing as the Biosphere</a> (meditations and flux data mandala) by Rosa Lewis and Maoya Bassiouni; <a href = "https://fluxnetart.github.io/enmass/">Soil</a> (musical score and audio) by Sara Bouchard; and <a href = "https://fluxnetart.github.io/18flux/">18//Flux</a> (single channel video) by Julia Oldham. The <a href ="https://prax.oregonstate.edu/visual-arts/past-exhibitions/currents-experiments-art-science-collaboration">Currents</a> group exhibition curated by Ashley Stull Meyers showcased five other art-science collaborations through the PRAx collaborative Faculty Fellowship program.

Visitors also had the pleasure to experience Agnes Meyer-Brandis' <a href = "https://www.periferia.helsinki.fi/home/agnes-meyer-brandis">Office for Tree Migration</a> (multichannel video and installation) in the Stirek gallery and <a href = "https://prax.oregonstate.edu/visual-arts/past-exhibitions/wandering-trees">Wandering Trees, Siikaneva Peatland</a> in the Toomey Lightboxes. These long-term artworks were developed in dialogue with the international scientific community at the Hyytiälä Forest Station under the framework of Climate Whirl Art&Science Program curated by Ulla Taipale.
