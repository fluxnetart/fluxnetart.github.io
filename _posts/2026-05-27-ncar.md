title: Experiencing Earth system science through art at NSF NCAR

categories: news
---

In May 2026, <i>flux</i>ART collaborated with NSF NCAR on a special public engagement event at the Mesa Lab in Boulder, Colorado, engaging both scientific and public audiences . 

As part of her NSF NCAR Faculty Fellowship, Maoya Bassiouni spoke in <a href = "https://edec.ucar.edu/events/fluxART">NCAR's Explorer Lecture Series</a> alongside artists Julia Oldham and Mallery Quetawki. Together, they shared how creative practice can deepen our relationship with ecosystem processes represented in NCAR’s Community Earth System Model. The event introduced audiences to FLUXNET’s global network of measurements of energy, water, and carbon fluxes, data that underpin climate modeling at NSF NCAR while also offering a creative invitation to feel and experience these same processes through art.

In addition to the lecture, the Mesa Lab hosted screenings of Julia Oldham’s <a href = "https://fluxnetart.github.io/orange/">September: Orange</a> on May 25 and 27, 2026. Julia's work offered a sensory and mysterious experience of long-term ecological data from the PhenoCam network, attended by approximately 90 people. 

Artworks and art-science didactics from all four <i>flux</i>ART projects are currently on view in the Mesa Lab Library. They will remain on exhibit from May 27 through December 31, 2026, offering an ongoing space to encounter flux science and data with a new creative curiosity.
