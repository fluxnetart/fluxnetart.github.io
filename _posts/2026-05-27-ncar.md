title: Experiencing Earth system science through art at NSF NCAR

excerpt: May 2026. The NSF NCAR Mesa lab showcased fluxART in a special Explorer Series event with artworks on view at the Mesa Lab Library through the end of the year.  
categories: news
---

In May 2026, <i>flux</i>ART collaborated with NSF NCAR <a href = "https://edec.ucar.edu/"> EdEC</a> on a special public event at the Mesa Lab in Boulder, Colorado, engaging both scientific and public audiences . 

As part of her NSF NCAR Faculty Fellowship, Maoya Bassiouni spoke in <a href = "https://edec.ucar.edu/events/fluxART">NSF NCAR's Explorer Lecture Series</a> alongside artists Julia Oldham and Mallery Quetawki. Together, they shared how creative practice can deepen our relationship with terrestrial ecosystem processes represented in NSF NCAR’s Community Earth System Model. The event introduced audiences to FLUXNET’s global network of land-atmosphere exchange observations, data that underpin water and carbon cycle modeling and research at NSF NCAR, while also offering a creative invitation to reflect on and experience these same processes through art.

In addition to the lecture, the Mesa Lab hosted screenings of Julia Oldham’s <a href = "https://fluxnetart.github.io/orange/">September: Orange</a> on May 26 and 27, 2026. Julia's work offered a sensory and mysterious experience of long-term ecological data from the PhenoCam network, attended by approximately 90 people. 

Artworks and art-science didactics from <i>flux</i>ART projects are currently on view in the <a href = "https://library.ucar.edu/about/locations">Mesa Lab Library</a>. They will remain on exhibit from May 27 through December 31, 2026, offering an ongoing space to encounter flux science and data with a new creative curiosity.
